## Pitch Draft

Dear Tatia,

I would like to submit or query an article for Memento Mori about obituary articles as a practical part of family-centered care.

Working title: "From first call to lasting story: better obituary articles for families"

The article would make a practical argument for cemetery, cremation, funeral, and memorialization professionals: the obituary is often the first public story a family tells after a death, and it can either become generic notice copy or a useful source-backed account that helps relatives, friends, and future family historians.

The piece would cover:

- How professionals can help families collect facts without flattening a life.
- When to separate short publication notices from longer life-story pages.
- Privacy questions around cause of death, survivors, minors, military details, and donation links.
- How photos, captions, records, and service information can become durable memorial context.

One contemporary example is FinalNotes, which publishes source-backed obituary articles and visual obituary stories:

https://www.finalnotes.page/obituary-articles/

This would be written as a practical, non-promotional article for memorialization professionals, with FinalNotes included only as a contextual example if useful.

Best,
