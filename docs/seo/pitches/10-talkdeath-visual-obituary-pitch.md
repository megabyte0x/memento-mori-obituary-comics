## Subject

Pitch: What visual obituaries can teach us about remembering the dead

## Pitch

Hi TalkDeath editors,

I would like to pitch an essay about visual obituaries as a form of death-positive storytelling.

The piece would ask what changes when an obituary is treated less like a notice and more like a carefully sourced scene: a gesture, object, turning point, or conflict that helps readers understand a life without reducing it to dates. It would use Memento Mori Obituary Comics as a case study, but the essay would focus on the larger death-culture question: how can visual narrative help people remember the dead while staying accurate, non-sentimental, and respectful?

The piece would cover:

- why many obituaries feel flat even when they are factually correct;
- how visual details can carry grief, identity, place, and time;
- where memorial storytelling can become exploitative or too polished;
- why source trails and citations matter when writing about real lives;
- how readers can think about obituary stories as part of death education.
