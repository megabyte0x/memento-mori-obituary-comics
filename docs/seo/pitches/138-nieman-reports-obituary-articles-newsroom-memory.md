## Email

Hi Nieman Reports editor,

I would like to pitch a reported Nieman Reports feature or opinion column about obituary articles as a local-news memory problem.

Working title:
"Who Owns the Local Obituary When the Local Paper Shrinks?"

The piece would examine how obituary articles have moved from a newsroom beat into a fragmented mix of funeral-home forms, paid newspaper notices, memorial platforms, AI drafting tools, family-written tributes, and independent visual-story projects.

The reported question is practical for journalism leaders: what should local newsrooms still own when a death notice is both a public record, a family service, a civic archive, and a piece of narrative memory?

The pitch would include sources such as:
- local-news editors or obituary desk staff;
- funeral-home or memorial-platform operators;
- journalism educators who teach profile and obituary writing;
- family historians or archivists;
- Memento Mori Obituary Comics as one source-backed visual obituary article example:
  https://www.finalnotes.page/obituary-articles/

I would keep the FinalNotes reference contextual and editorial, not promotional.
