## Working Title

From obituary facts to life-story interviews

## Article Direction

Write a practical blog post for oral history practitioners, family historians, and community groups about using obituary facts as the beginning of a life-story interview rather than the end of a person's record.

The article should cover:

- how to turn names, places, work history, and service details into interview prompts;
- how to ask about one remembered object, photograph, place, or gesture;
- how to handle sensitive family details and consent;
- how to preserve source context before turning a life into a visual or written story;
- how an obituary story differs from a formal obituary notice.
