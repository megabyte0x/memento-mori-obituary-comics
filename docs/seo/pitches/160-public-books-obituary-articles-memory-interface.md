## Pitch Draft

Dear Public Books editors,

I would like to pitch an essay on obituary articles as public interfaces for memory.

Why this intervention is necessary: obituaries are often treated as formulaic notices, but they now sit at the intersection of local journalism, funeral-home platforms, genealogy databases, AI drafting tools, visual storytelling, and family-written public memory. That makes the obituary article a useful form for thinking about evidence, privacy, authorship, and what a culture thinks a life is allowed to mean.

Why now: as local obituary desks shrink and memorial platforms expand, families increasingly inherit editorial decisions once handled by newspapers. The essay would ask what is lost when the form becomes either template copy or SEO debris, and what is gained when it becomes a careful, source-backed narrative.
