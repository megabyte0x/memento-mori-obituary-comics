## Title

The Thing We Chose After the Obituary Was Written

## Submission Direction

Submit only if a real human writer can produce a polished personal essay from lived experience.

The essay should not pitch the company or ask for a backlink. It should stand on its own as a vulnerable narrative about the moment an obituary became something more specific: a remembered object, a family image, a sentence, a scene, or a hard choice about what to leave out.

If contributor notes allow a contextual link, use:
https://www.finalnotes.page/obituary-stories/

## Essay Shape

Open in a concrete scene: a table, folder, phone call, photograph, printed obituary proof, funeral program, or family text thread.

Move into the central choice:

- Which detail felt true enough to keep?
- Which detail felt too private?
- Who had the right to decide?
- What did the final story preserve that the notice could not?

End with restraint. The strongest fit for The Sun is a human essay about grief, memory, and truth, not an explanation of a service.
