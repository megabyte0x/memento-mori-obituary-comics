## Pitch Draft

Hi Design Week editorial team,

I would like to pitch a concise column or project story about designing obituary articles for memory, evidence, and dignity.

The piece would use Memento Mori Obituary Comics as one working example of a broader design question: when families, editors, artists, and digital publishers turn a life into a public obituary article, what should the page preserve beyond names and dates?

Context page:

https://www.finalnotes.page/obituary-articles/

The argument would be practical and design-led:

- Obituary pages are public interfaces for grief, memory, citation, and search.
- Visual storytelling can help, but only when captions, source trails, privacy choices, and page structure are handled carefully.
- A good obituary article should make the life legible without making private details unnecessarily public.
