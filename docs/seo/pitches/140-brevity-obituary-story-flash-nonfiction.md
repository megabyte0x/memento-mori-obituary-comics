## Submission Direction

Submit only a human-written, unpublished flash nonfiction essay of 750 words or fewer. Do not submit AI-generated or AI-assisted copy.

Working title:
"The Object We Put in the Last Panel"

The essay should focus on one true, specific moment: the family choosing the object, room, gesture, photo, or line that makes an obituary story feel like a life instead of a notice.

FinalNotes should not be the subject of a promotional essay. If accepted and if contributor-note policy allows, the author bio can mention Memento Mori Obituary Comics or the visual obituary stories page:
https://www.finalnotes.page/obituary-stories/

## Human Draft Prompt

Write from one concrete moment. Keep the piece under 750 words. Avoid advice, explanation, or product language. The emotional movement should come from the scene itself.
