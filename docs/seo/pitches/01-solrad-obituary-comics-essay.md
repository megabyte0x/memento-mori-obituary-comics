## Subject

Pitch: Obituary comics as source-backed visual biography

## Email

Hi SOLRAD editors,

I would like to pitch a roughly 1,000-word essay for SOLRAD on obituary comics as a form of source-backed visual biography.

The piece would not be a product announcement. It would use Memento Mori Obituary Comics as a working case study to examine a broader comics question: what changes when an obituary becomes sequential art? The essay would look at how panels can carry memory, how captions and source trails keep the work accountable, and how death, illness, exile, and grief become legible without reducing a life to a checklist.

Working title:
"What Happens When an Obituary Becomes a Comic?"

Angle:
Obituaries are usually treated as prose notices or memorial records. Comics can do something different: they can choose a pressure point in a life, show time and consequence, and still preserve a factual evidence layer around the artwork. I would frame this through visual biography, grief comics, and the responsibilities of drawing from documented lives.
