## Pitch Draft

Hi Hyperallergic editors,

I would like to pitch an opinion essay on how obituary stories are becoming visual memory work.

The piece would argue that contemporary obituary storytelling is no longer only a newspaper notice or funeral-home template. Families, artists, archivists, and digital memorial projects are turning ordinary evidence - photos, captions, objects, dates, records, and fragments of speech - into public visual narratives. The essay would connect this shift to broader questions in art and memory: who gets pictured, what evidence is enough, and how a life can be honored without flattening it into iconography.
