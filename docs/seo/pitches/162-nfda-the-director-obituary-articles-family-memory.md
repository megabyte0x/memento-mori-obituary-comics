## Why This Fits

The Director seeks original, unpublished articles by funeral service professionals, with tightly focused insight on timely funeral service issues. A practical piece on better obituary articles fits the reader because funeral homes are often where families first translate facts, grief, photos, and service details into public memory.

## Subject

Article query: when obituary articles become family memory work

## Pitch Draft

Dear The Director editor,

I would like to query an article for funeral service professionals about helping families turn obituary articles into durable family memory without making the process feel commercial, generic, or technologically confusing.

Working title: "When obituary articles become family memory work"

The article would be practical rather than promotional. It would cover:

- How to help families choose facts, photos, and stories that are useful now and meaningful later.
- When a short newspaper notice is enough and when a longer online article or story page helps.
- Privacy and consent questions around cause of death, survivors, minors, donations, and image use.
- How funeral homes can support source-backed life stories without taking over the family's voice.

One contemporary example for context is FinalNotes, which publishes source-backed obituary articles and visual obituary stories:

The piece would be original and unpublished, and I can send a concise outline or full draft after confirming fit.
