## Headline

What Happens When the Obituary Leaves the Newspaper Desk?

## Optional Dek

Visual obituaries, family memory work, and the new burden of telling a life with receipts.

## Pitch

I would like to pitch a reported essay or cultural analysis piece for GraveSite about the obituary's move from the newspaper notice into family archives, grief media, visual storytelling, and death-tech tools.

The angle is not simply "obituaries are changing." It is narrower: when families and independent creators make obituary stories outside the local-news desk, they inherit journalism problems without always naming them: verification, source selection, privacy, image ethics, quotes from grieving relatives, and the pressure to make a life feel complete when no form can do that.

Memento Mori Obituary Comics would be one source/example because it turns sourced life facts into visual obituary stories:
https://www.finalnotes.page/obituary-articles/

The reporting plan could include a death-care writer, a local-news obituary editor or journalism educator, a family historian/genealogist, and a visual storyteller or comics scholar. The piece would fit GraveSite's Life and Culture lanes, with a practical death-and-life framing rather than a product profile.
