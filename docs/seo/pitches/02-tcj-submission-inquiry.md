## Subject

Submission Inquiry: obituary comics as a comics form

## Email

Hi TCJ editors,

I would like to pitch a critical essay for TCJ.com about obituary comics as a comics form.

The proposed piece would use Memento Mori Obituary Comics as a case study, but the argument is broader: obituaries usually compress a life into a prose notice, while comics can sequence memory, mortality, evidence, and interpretation in ways prose cannot. The essay would ask what the medium does well in this space, where it can become sentimental or irresponsible, and what a source-backed page needs around the art for the work to be legible as biography rather than tribute art.

Working title:
"The Obituary as Comics Form"

Why it may fit TCJ:
The piece is comics-first rather than promotional. It is about form, ethics, evidence, and the craft problem of turning documented lives into sequential art. The current archive includes visual obituary stories for Aleksandr Solzhenitsyn, Primo Levi, Henri Matisse, Harriet Tubman, Stephen Hawking, Louis Zamperini, Frida Kahlo, Viktor Frankl, and Dostoyevsky.
