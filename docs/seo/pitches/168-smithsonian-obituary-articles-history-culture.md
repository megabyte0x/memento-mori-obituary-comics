## Pitch Subject

How obituary articles became public memory infrastructure

## 250-Word Form Draft

Obituary articles are often treated as routine notices, but they have become one of the most durable public records many people leave behind. I would like to report a History/Arts & Culture feature on how the obituary evolved from newspaper announcement to family archive, genealogy source, digital memorial, and visual biography.

The piece would trace the form through historical obituary writing, newspaper death notices, family-history research, online memorial platforms, and contemporary experiments that turn verified facts, objects, photographs, and captions into fuller life stories. The central question is cultural: when a person dies, who decides which facts become public memory, and how does the format shape what future readers can know?

The reporting would include historians, archivists, obituary writers, genealogists, and artists working with memorial images or source-backed life stories. FinalNotes would appear only as one current example of visual obituary storytelling:

https://www.finalnotes.page/obituary-articles/

The story would be timely because families increasingly rely on digital records, search engines, and memorial sites, while local newspapers and funeral-home templates shape how lives are preserved online.
