## Subject

Pitch: When obituary stories become community memory work

## Email

Hi Current editors,

I would like to pitch a reported piece for Current about how public media organizations can treat obituary stories as community memory work, especially in places where local obituary desks, community newspapers, and family archives are shrinking.

The story would be written for public media professionals, not as a product announcement. It would examine how stations and community partners can help families preserve source-backed life stories while respecting privacy, consent, and local history.

Possible angle:
"When obituary stories become community memory work"

Reporting could include:

- public media producers or engagement leads working with community archives;
- local historians, librarians, or oral-history teams;
- families deciding what belongs in public record versus private memory;
- examples of visual obituary or narrative obituary work, including Memento Mori Obituary Comics as one source-backed example:
