## Why This Fits

Scholastic Upfront published an "Overlooked No More" lesson plan that asks students to research and write an obituary. The FinalNotes lesson is a supplemental resource for visual obituary examples and source-backed obituary article structure.

Hi Scholastic Upfront team,

I found the Upfront "Overlooked No More" lesson plan while researching classroom resources where students research and write obituaries.

One supplemental resource that may fit teachers using that lesson is this obituary lesson plan:

https://www.finalnotes.page/obituary-lesson-plan/

It gives an 80-90 minute classroom sequence for comparing death notices, eulogies, reported obituary articles, and visual obituaries. It also includes an assessment checklist and source-backed visual obituary examples, which may help students see how research becomes a readable life story.

If useful, it could fit as an additional resource for teachers adapting obituary writing, research, ELA, or media-literacy lessons.
