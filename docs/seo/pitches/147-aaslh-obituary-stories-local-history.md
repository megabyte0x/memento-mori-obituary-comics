## Working Title

Obituary stories as local history infrastructure

## Article Direction

This should be a short practical article for state and local history practitioners. It should explain how local history organizations, archives, libraries, and small museums can help families turn obituary facts into source-backed life stories without overstepping privacy or consent.

The article should offer concrete takeaways:

- use obituary drafts as prompts for oral-history follow-up;
- preserve source trails for photographs, letters, service records, and newspaper clippings;
- separate public facts from private family context;
- invite families to identify one scene, object, or place that makes the life locally legible;
- keep visual obituary stories grounded in evidence.
