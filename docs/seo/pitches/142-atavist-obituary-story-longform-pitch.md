Hi Atavist editors,

I would like to pitch a reported longform story for The Atavist about the making of one source-backed visual obituary.

Working title:
"The Last Panel"

The piece would follow one family, writer, or artist through the narrative problem behind an obituary story: how do you decide which object, scene, document, or remembered gesture can carry the weight of a life without turning that life into a tribute template?

The reporting would center on people, documents, and process, not a product announcement. Memento Mori Obituary Comics would be one source-backed example:
https://www.finalnotes.page/obituary-stories/

Possible reporting threads:

- interviews with family members, artists, or obituary writers;
- source documents, photos, letters, and public records behind the story;
- the ethical line between compression and invention;
- what changes when an obituary story is built visually.

I would be happy to send a fuller reporting plan, source-access notes, clips, and a brief author bio.
