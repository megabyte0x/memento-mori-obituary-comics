## Why This Fits

CJR asks for pitches that engage contemporary concerns in journalism and explain how the press benefits from the story. This should be a journalism criticism pitch about obituary coverage, local news, and visual formats, not a backlink request.

## Subject

Pitch: What obituary articles reveal about local-news memory work

## Message

Hi CJR editors,

Pitch: What obituary articles reveal about local-news memory work.

The story would examine why obituary articles still matter as journalism: they combine verification, source sensitivity, family memory, public record, and the newsroom responsibility to make a life legible to people who never met the subject.

I would use Memento Mori Obituary Comics as one example of a source-backed visual obituary format, then report outward: obituary writers, journalism educators, and local-news editors who treat obituaries as more than paid notices.

Reference page:
https://www.finalnotes.page/obituary-articles/

Why now: as local newsrooms shrink and families publish more memorial content directly, the line between death notice, tribute, profile, and reported obituary article is getting blurrier. A media-focused piece could ask what standards still matter when obituary storytelling moves across newspapers, newsletters, memorial sites, and visual formats.
