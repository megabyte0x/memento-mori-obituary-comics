## Subject

Pitch: What visual obituaries can teach us about remembering the dead

## Pitch Message

Hi TalkDeath editors,

I would like to pitch a human-written essay about visual obituaries as a form of death-positive storytelling.

The piece would ask what changes when an obituary is treated less like a notice and more like a carefully sourced scene: a gesture, object, turning point, conflict, or remembered place that helps readers understand a life without reducing it to dates. Memento Mori Obituary Comics would be a disclosed case study, but the essay would focus on the larger death-culture question: how can visual narrative help people remember the dead while staying accurate, non-sentimental, and respectful?

The essay would cover why many obituaries feel flat even when they are factually correct, how visual details can carry grief and identity, where memorial storytelling can become too polished or extractive, and why source trails matter when writing about real lives.
