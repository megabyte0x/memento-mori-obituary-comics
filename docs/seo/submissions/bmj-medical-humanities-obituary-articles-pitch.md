## Core Ask

Pitch a short critical guest post about obituary articles, illness narrative, narrative closure, consent, and public/private memory. Do not pitch a product or generic resource roundup.

## Proposed Article

Title: `After the chart goes quiet: obituary articles and the ethics of narrative closure`

Target length: 700-800 words.

Angle: Obituary articles often convert clinical facts and family memory into public narrative. The essay should ask how writers can preserve dignity and narrative complexity while respecting consent, privacy, ambiguity, and grief.

## Proposed Structure

1. Opening scene: a family has official facts, but no story yet.
2. Medical humanities frame: illness narrative, public memory, and narrative closure.
3. Ethical tension: what to include, what to omit, who gets to decide.
4. Practical example: visual obituary storytelling as one method for preserving evidence and context.
5. Closing claim: an obituary article should not force certainty where a life needs care, privacy, and texture.

## Do Not Send Until

- A qualified human author owns the argument.
- The article is not promotional.
- The FinalNotes link is used only as a contextual example.
- Any case detail is permissioned, anonymized, or hypothetical.
- A rights-cleared image is available if included.
