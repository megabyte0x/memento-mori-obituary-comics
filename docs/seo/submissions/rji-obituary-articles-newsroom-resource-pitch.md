The partnership page says RJI projects should tackle journalism needs, challenges, and gaps; be actionable, useful, free to utilize, and open source; and benefit community-centered small and medium newsrooms and journalists.

## Inquiry

Subject: Partnership idea: a free obituary-story kit for small newsrooms

Hi RJI Innovation team,

I would like to ask whether this could fit RJI's partnership-project lane: a free, open newsroom resource kit for small and medium newsrooms that want to improve obituary articles and community memory work.

Working title:
"The Community Obituary Story Kit"

The resource would help local journalists, student newsrooms, and community editors decide when a death notice should become a fuller obituary article, how to verify family-provided details, how to avoid privacy mistakes, and how to preserve a richer civic record without adding a heavy editorial burden.

Memento Mori Obituary Comics could serve as one visual/source-backed example of how obituary facts become narrative structure:
https://www.finalnotes.page/obituary-articles/

The final resource would need to be useful beyond FinalNotes. Possible outputs:
- obituary article decision tree;
- interview/source checklist;
- family privacy checklist;
- examples of text-first and visual obituary formats;
- classroom/newsroom exercise for profile and obituary writing.
