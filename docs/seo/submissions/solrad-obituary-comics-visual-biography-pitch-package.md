Hi SOLRAD editors,

I would like to pitch a roughly 1,000-word essay for SOLRAD on obituary comics as source-backed visual biography.

Working title: What Happens When an Obituary Becomes a Comic?

Proposed topic and angle:
Obituaries are usually treated as prose notices, memorial records, or short life summaries. Comics can do something different. They can sequence memory, evidence, place, gesture, silence, illness, exile, and grief in ways that prose often compresses. The essay would ask what changes when an obituary becomes sequential art, and what an artist owes the subject when the life being drawn is documented rather than invented.

The piece would use Memento Mori Obituary Comics as one working case study, but the argument is broader and comics-first. It would look at how panels can carry time and consequence, how captions and source trails keep the work accountable, and where visual obituary storytelling can become too sentimental, too illustrative, or too loose with facts.
