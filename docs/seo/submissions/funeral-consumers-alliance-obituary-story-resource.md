## Why This Qualifies

Funeral Consumers Alliance is a national consumer organization focused on funeral planning, consumer rights, and practical education. Its current planning guidance tells families to start funeral planning at home, make choices based on what is meaningful rather than expected, and put plans in writing. The site lists `fca@funerals.org` as the public contact email.

The best fit is a free, noncommercial obituary-writing planning resource for families. This should not be framed as a backlink request. The editorial/resource value is that obituary writing is one of the practical family tasks adjacent to funeral planning, memorial services, privacy decisions, and putting wishes in writing.

Hi Funeral Consumers Alliance team,

I found FCA while looking for consumer-facing funeral planning resources. One free resource that may be useful for families handling the writing side of funeral planning is this obituary story guide:

https://www.finalnotes.page/how-to-write-an-obituary-story/

It focuses on practical, non-commercial steps: gathering verified facts, choosing a specific remembered scene, separating evidence from tribute, protecting privacy, and writing an obituary or memorial article that is useful to both family and readers.

There is also a printable worksheet for families who need to collect details before drafting:

https://www.finalnotes.page/obituary-story-worksheet/

Because FCA already encourages families to talk through funeral decisions, write plans down, and choose meaningful arrangements without unnecessary pressure or cost, this may fit as a supplemental writing resource for families preparing obituary or memorial text.
