## Why This Qualifies

Current serves people working in public radio, television, networks, and production companies. Its writing guidelines ask for stories that help public media professionals understand developments affecting their work, including audience service, engagement, technology, community needs, and public media practice. A reported story about obituary articles as community memory work can fit if it is grounded in public media sources and avoids self-promotion.

## Draft Pitch Angle

Working title: "When obituary stories become community memory work"

The piece examines whether public media organizations can help communities preserve life stories as local obituary desks shrink. It should focus on audience service, archival responsibility, consent, and practical workflows for families and local institutions.
