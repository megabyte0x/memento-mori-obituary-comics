## Why This Qualifies

The International Storytelling Center's current story-collection page asks people to explain why storytelling matters. It says selected statements may be featured on ISC's website, in rotating story exhibits, and eventually housed in Library of Congress archives. That makes it a relevant authority surface for obituary stories as memory preservation, but it is not a guaranteed backlink route.

## Final Submit Checklist

- Use a real human name and email.
- Write a short, personal 1-2 sentence statement.
- Record a human audio version of the statement.
- Choose a relevant photo only if rights and consent are clear.
- Include the FinalNotes URL only if the form provides a natural website/context field.
- Do not ask for anchor text, link treatment, or SEO value.

## Statement Direction

The strongest statement should connect obituary stories to memory preservation:

- an obituary as public record;
- a story as a way to keep the person recognizable;
- the power of one specific image, object, or remembered line;
- the responsibility to tell the truth without exposing private family details.
