## Why This Qualifies

Los Angeles Public Library's History & Genealogy department maintains genealogy collections, newspaper resources, obituary research guidance, and staff-created research guides. Its department page says staff can answer quick ready-reference questions and lists an "Obituaries in Los Angeles County" research guide. Its genealogy page points patrons toward obituary research.

The best fit is a post-research writing aid for patrons who have already found an obituary, death notice, or newspaper clue and want to preserve the story responsibly. This should not be framed as replacing LAPL's local-history or obituary-search guidance.

Hi History & Genealogy team,

I found the LAPL genealogy guide and its obituary research resources while looking for library resources that help patrons work with obituary records.

One related resource that may be useful after patrons locate an obituary or death notice is a printable obituary story worksheet:

https://www.finalnotes.page/obituary-story-worksheet/

The worksheet helps people move from a located record into a careful family-history narrative. It separates verified facts from remembered details, asks for source notes, and includes prompts for choosing one scene or object that can carry the story without adding unsupported claims.

The companion guide is here:

https://www.finalnotes.page/how-to-write-an-obituary-story/
