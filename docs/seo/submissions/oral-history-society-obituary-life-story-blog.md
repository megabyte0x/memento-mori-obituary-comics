## Why This Qualifies

The Oral History Society blog asks contributors to share ideas and experiences in an informal, supportive way. Its guidelines request a title, featured image, summary, author bio, and article, and it allows relevant links and suggested texts/articles/websites for further exploration.

This is a strong topical fit because obituary stories often begin with oral-history questions: what happened, who remembers it, what evidence exists, and what should remain private.

## Final Submit Checklist

- Write the article as a fully human contribution.
- Include a clear title.
- Include a short summary.
- Include author name and a 2-3 sentence bio.
- Include a featured image with permissions, attribution, and caption.
- Keep the article 500-1,000 words.
- Include links only where they help readers.
- Do not ask for anchor text, followed links, or SEO value.

## Draft Article Outline

Working title: "From obituary facts to life-story interviews"

1. Explain why an obituary is often the start, not the finish, of life-story work.
2. Show how to convert obituary facts into interview prompts.
3. Discuss consent, privacy, and emotional care.
4. Offer a simple source-backed workflow for family and community groups.
5. Close with useful links, including the FinalNotes obituary stories page if appropriate.
