## Why This Qualifies

AASLH says its blog is for history practitioners and connects people preserving and interpreting state and local history. The current submission page welcomes short original articles at any time, especially articles with practical takeaways for history organizations and staff.

This target fits if the article is framed around local history practice, not a product announcement.

## Final Submit Checklist

- Confirm a qualified author, role, and organization context.
- Keep the article 350-800 words.
- Provide at least one landscape image with permissions, caption, and credit.
- Use Chicago style where relevant.
- Use URLs for references; do not use footnotes.
- Make the takeaways useful for local history organizations.
- Include FinalNotes only as a supporting example or reference.
- Do not request a backlink, anchor text, or follow status.

## Draft Article Outline

Working title: "Obituary stories as local history infrastructure"
