## Why This Qualifies

The Sun publishes personal essays, short stories, poems, and black-and-white photography in print and online. Its submission page asks for narrative writing that is vulnerable, difficult, reflective, and human. That makes it a fit only for a fully human essay about the moment an obituary becomes a more specific life story.

This should not be sent as a pitch about a product, a backlink request, or a generated article.

## Draft Premise

Working title: "The Thing We Chose After the Obituary Was Written"

The essay begins after the formal obituary facts are already settled. The narrator and family still have to decide which single image, object, or line tells the truth of the person they lost. The piece becomes a meditation on the difference between public notice and private memory.
