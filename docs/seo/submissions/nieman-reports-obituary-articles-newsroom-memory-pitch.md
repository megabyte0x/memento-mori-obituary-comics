## Why This Qualifies

Nieman Reports is a high-authority journalism target if the pitch is about the future of local news, newsroom practice, civic memory, and journalism leadership. Its pitch guide welcomes deeply reported feature stories with insight and analysis on the challenges and opportunities facing journalism today, and it also publishes opinion columns.

The strongest angle is not "please link to FinalNotes." The strongest angle is a reported story about how obituary articles are changing as local-news capacity shrinks and families, funeral homes, memorial platforms, and independent story formats fill the gap.

## Email

Subject: Pitch for Nieman Reports

Hi Nieman Reports editor,

I would like to pitch a reported feature or opinion column about obituary articles as a local-news memory problem.

Working title:
"Who Owns the Local Obituary When the Local Paper Shrinks?"

The piece would examine how obituary articles have moved from a newsroom beat into a fragmented mix of funeral-home forms, paid newspaper notices, memorial platforms, AI drafting tools, family-written tributes, and independent visual-story projects.

The practical question for journalism leaders is this: what should local newsrooms still own when a death notice is both a public record, a family service, a civic archive, and a piece of narrative memory?

The reporting plan would include:
- local-news editors or obituary desk staff;
- funeral-home or memorial-platform operators;
- journalism educators who teach profile and obituary writing;
- family historians or archivists;
- Memento Mori Obituary Comics as one source-backed visual obituary article example:
  https://www.finalnotes.page/obituary-articles/

I would keep the FinalNotes reference contextual and editorial, not promotional.

Best,
