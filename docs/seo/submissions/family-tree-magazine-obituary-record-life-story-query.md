## Query Draft

Hi [editor name],

I would like to query a practical article for Family Tree Magazine: "From Obituary Record to Sourced Life Story."

The piece would help researchers who find an obituary but are not sure how to preserve the citation, check names and dates, separate evidence from family memory, and turn the notice into a narrative paragraph without overclaiming.

Proposed structure:

1. Save the citation before writing anything.
2. Identify which details are evidence, which are clues, and which need confirmation.
3. Use local-library, newspaper-room, and microfilm workflows when the online result is incomplete.
4. Keep sensitive details out of public family-history drafts when living relatives may be affected.
5. Convert the record into a short sourced life-story paragraph.
