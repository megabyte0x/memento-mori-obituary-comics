## Proposed Article Outline If Accepted

Title:
From Obituary Record to Family History Story

Summary paragraph:
An obituary can be more than a date-and-survivor notice, but it should not become fiction. This article shows how family historians can transcribe an obituary, separate evidence from interpretation, use public records to fill context, and turn verified clues into a readable family-history story.

Sections:

1. Start with a clean transcription.
2. Mark every fact before writing a scene.
3. Separate public evidence from family interpretation.
4. Use records to test place, occupation, migration, family structure, and date claims.
5. Add context without inventing private motives.
6. Preserve citations for future researchers.
7. Decide whether a visual or narrative retelling is appropriate.
