## Why This Qualifies

History@Work publishes original posts about issues in public history, typically 700-1,000 words. Its guidelines ask for pitches of one to three paragraphs, not full drafts, and expect posts to be accessible, evidence-based, and informed by public-history practice.

This is a strong fit only if framed as public history, not grief marketing or SEO outreach.

## Final Send Checklist

## Draft Pitch Angle

Working title: "When obituary articles become public history"

The proposed post argues that obituary articles are not just family announcements. They are small acts of public interpretation that can preserve everyday lives, especially where local news and community-history infrastructure have weakened. The article should discuss consent, documentation, family memory, and the line between tribute and public record.
