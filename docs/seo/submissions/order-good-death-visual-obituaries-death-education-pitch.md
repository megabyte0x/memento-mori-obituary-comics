## Subject

Pitch: What visual obituaries reveal about how we remember the dead

## Email

Hi Sarah,

I would like to pitch an 800-1,000 word article for The Order of the Good Death about visual obituaries, memorial art, and death education.

The idea is not "here is a project." The essay would ask a broader question: when an obituary becomes sequential art, what does that reveal about the way people remember, simplify, beautify, or avoid the dead? Obituaries often compress a life into a neat prose notice. Visual storytelling can do something more complicated: it can show a moment, a contradiction, a body under pressure, a place, or an object that makes mortality harder to abstract.

The piece would use obituary comics as one example of how art can challenge the dead-as-summary habit. It would also discuss where memorial art can become too sentimental, too clean, or too extractive, and why factual source trails matter when a real person's life is being drawn. I can provide relevant writing samples and a short author bio before drafting.
