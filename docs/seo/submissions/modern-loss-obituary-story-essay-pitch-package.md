## Form-Ready Pitch

Working title:
"How an Obituary Becomes a Visual Biography"

I would like to pitch a 700- to 800-word essay for Modern Loss about what changes when an obituary becomes a visual biography.

The piece would be tightly focused on one grief-writing problem: after a death, families and writers often have facts, photos, and memories, but no obvious way to choose the one scene that makes a life feel specific. A visual obituary can help by making the writer choose a gesture, object, place, turning point, or contradiction instead of flattening the person into a list of dates and roles.

The essay would use Memento Mori Obituary Comics as the working example, but the focus would be reader-facing and candid rather than promotional: how images can carry memory, how captions and source trails keep a tribute honest, and why an obituary story can feel more humane when it admits what it cannot fully summarize.
