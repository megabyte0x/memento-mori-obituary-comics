Ask whether source-backed visual obituary stories could fit a StoryCenter case-study conversation, story-practice resource, program conversation, or editorial reference. Do not ask for a backlink. Any link should be an editorially useful context link only.

## One-Paragraph Pitch

Memento Mori Obituary Comics creates source-backed visual obituary stories that combine interviews, records, captions, and selected images into short visual life stories. The project may be useful to StoryCenter audiences as a practical example of digital storytelling around memory, family archives, grief, and ethical consent. The request is to ask whether a specific consent-backed story or project reflection would fit StoryCenter's programs, publications, stories, or resource conversations.

## Materials To Attach Or Link

- Short project note with specific story owner and why this story matters.
- Rights and consent statement.
- Source/citation summary.
- One or two preview images only if rights are confirmed.
- Link for context: `https://www.finalnotes.page/obituary-stories/`

## Do Not Send Until

- A real project owner is named.
- Permission is documented for any family story, image, or source excerpt.
- The sender can answer follow-up questions about ethics, method, and story process.
- The message is personalized to one StoryCenter program or publication surface.
