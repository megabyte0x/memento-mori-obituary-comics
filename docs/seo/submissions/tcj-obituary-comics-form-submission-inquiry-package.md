Hi TCJ editors,

I would like to pitch a critical essay for TCJ.com about obituary comics as a comics form.

Working title: The Obituary as Comics Form

Topic:
The proposed piece would use Memento Mori Obituary Comics as one case study, but the argument is broader: obituaries usually compress a life into a prose notice, while comics can sequence memory, mortality, evidence, and interpretation in ways prose cannot. The essay would ask what the medium does well in this space, where it can become sentimental or irresponsible, and what source-backed context a page needs around the art for the work to be legible as biography rather than tribute illustration.

Why it may fit TCJ:
The piece is comics-first. It is about form, ethics, evidence, and the craft problem of turning documented lives into sequential art. It would treat obituary comics alongside biography comics, grief comics, and nonfiction comics rather than as a product announcement.
