## Why This Qualifies

Nieman Storyboard is one of the strongest authority targets for the obituary-articles lane because it focuses on nonfiction storytelling craft, narrative journalism, production, editing, ethics, and the people behind story work. The pitch guide welcomes professionals and students and specifically names Q&As, annotations, craft analysis, interviews, and personal essays or analysis about nonfiction craft or culture.

The best ask is a craft-focused essay or Q&A about visual obituary articles as nonfiction compression, not a backlink request.

## Email

Subject: Pitch for Nieman Storyboard: what visual obituaries teach about narrative craft

Hi Nieman Storyboard editor,

I would like to pitch a Story Craft essay or Q&A for Nieman Storyboard about what visual obituary articles reveal about nonfiction structure.

Working title:
"What Visual Obituaries Teach About the Shape of a Life Story"

The piece would examine one craft problem that obituary writers, journalism students, and narrative nonfiction editors all face: how do you compress a life into a truthful story without reducing the person to a list of roles, dates, and virtues?

The reporting/craft angle would use Memento Mori Obituary Comics as one example of source-backed visual obituary storytelling:
https://www.finalnotes.page/obituary-articles/

The piece could be structured as either:
- a Story Craft essay on scene selection, evidence trails, captions, and the ethics of compression; or
- a Strictly Q&A-style conversation with a journalist, obituary editor, or comics scholar about what changes when an obituary is built visually.

I would keep the focus on nonfiction craft and ethics, not on promotion.

Best,
[sender name]
